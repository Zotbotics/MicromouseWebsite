Micromouse @ UCI

An IEEE & Zotbotics Collaboration.

What is Micromouse?

Micromouse is a yearly challenge put on by IEEE on the national level, testing the problem solving and ingenuity of university students around the world! The task is simple: create a small 'mouse' robot that can traverse a maze and solve it consistently in as short a time as possible. The challenge: making that actually happen!

The Challenge

What Do We Do?

As a group, we tackle that challenge and take the hard abstract goal of making a 'maze solving robot' into manageable steps for engineers of all levels. Our Micromouse veterans help teach the beginners as well as working on improving their own designs. Tired of just learning about making things, come to Micromouse and actually make something! We keep have a blog with all of our meetings, so check that out.

Logistics (FAQ)

Want some more details? We group into teams of 2-4 people for competition, and require a $20 Micromouse fee to participate (in addition to a $30 IEEE fee if you aren't already a member). A beginner mouse can cost around $100-$150 split among the entire team. If cost is an issue contact us! Read the full information session meeting notes for other FAQs.
